INITIALISING SENTINEL

🇸🇬 DSTA Mission Alignment

DSTA (Defence Science and Technology Agency) drives innovation across Systems Engineering, Cyber Defence, C4ISTAR, and Digital Platforms to strengthen Singapore's defence capabilities. SENTINEL directly supports this mission by reducing decision latency in intelligence workflows — transforming raw multi-source data into structured, assessed intelligence within seconds. Built for the Young Defence Scientists Programme (YDSP), SENTINEL demonstrates how AI agents can augment defence analysts, cyber operators, and systems engineers in their operational roles.

SECTION 01
The Challenge
Why defence intelligence analysis is broken — and how SENTINEL fixes it
⏱️
Multi-Source Overload
DSTA engineers and SAF operators process intelligence, threat feeds, sensor data, and programme updates across multiple domains simultaneously. The volume exceeds human capacity in time-critical scenarios.
✅
Structured Intelligence in Seconds
SENTINEL accepts raw, unstructured input across any domain and returns formatted INTSUMs, SITREPs, and priority-flagged assessments — cutting processing time from minutes to seconds.
🔍
Inconsistent Analytical Output
Manual intelligence structuring varies by individual, shift, and workload. Inconsistent formats slow downstream decision-making and increase the risk of missed correlations in cyber and C4ISTAR operations.
✅
Rigorous, Repeatable Formats
SENTINEL enforces standardised INTSUM, SITREP, and CCIR outputs with explicit confidence ratings on every finding — ensuring consistency across all intelligence products regardless of operator or time of day.
⚠️
Decision Latency Under Pressure
In DCCOM CTG and C4ISTAR ops, every minute spent sorting incoming reports is a minute not acting. Correlating disparate events — a cyber breach, an unknown track, a weather change — is cognitively demanding.
✅
Auto-Correlation & Threat Flagging
SENTINEL automatically cross-correlates concurrent events, flags patterns (e.g., diversionary overflight during data exfiltration), and generates CCIR — giving commanders decision-ready intelligence.

SECTION 04
External Skills
Plug-and-play capabilities that extend SENTINEL into new operational domains
📡
Threat Feed Parser
Reads security news feeds from around the world and picks out what's relevant to Singapore's defence — like having someone skim 100 newspapers and hand you the 2 articles that matter. Sources: AlienVault OTX, MISP, open-source threat reports.
🔍
IOC Extractor
You dump a messy hacker report into SENTINEL — it picks out the useful forensic bits: IP addresses, malware filenames, suspicious domains, cryptographic hashes. Hands you back a clean, organised list ready for SOC action. Outputs in STIX format.
🎣
Phishing Triage
Forward a suspicious email to SENTINEL — it tells you what's wrong with it: bad links, fake sender addresses, dangerous attachments, and social engineering red flags — before anyone clicks.
🩹
Vulnerability Brief
Tell SENTINEL a software name and version — it produces a structured briefing: what the vulnerability does, which systems are affected, whether exploits exist in the wild, and recommended mitigations. No manual research needed.
🧩 Skill architecture: Each skill has its own SKILL.md defining purpose, inputs, outputs, and safety constraints. Skills are independently add/remove without core changes.
